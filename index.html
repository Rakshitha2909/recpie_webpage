RASAM

INGREDENTS

RASAM POWDER:

  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies

BASE INGREDENTS

  • 1 medium-sized tomato (chopped or mashed)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (or small lemon-sized tamarind soaked in water)
  • ½ cup toor dal (pigeon peas) (cooked & mashed) (Optional)
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed, optional)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp rasam powder (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • Salt to taste.
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

TADKA

  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil

INSTRUCTIONS

  • Step 1: Prepare Rasam Powder (Skip if using store-bought)
    1. Dry roast black pepper, cumin, coriander seeds, and red chilies in a pan for 2 minutes.
    2. Grind them into a fine powder. Your homemade rasam powder is ready!
  • Step 2: Cook Dal (Skip if not using dal)
    1. Cook ½ cup toor dal in a pressure cooker with 1.5 cups of water until soft.
    2. Mash the dal and set it aside.
  • Step 3: Prepare the Rasam Base
    1. In a pot, add chopped tomato, tamarind extract, turmeric, salt, and 2 cups of water.
    2. Let it simmer for 5–7 minutes until tomatoes turn soft.
    3. Add rasam powder, cumin powder, black pepper powder, and garlic (if using).
    4. Add the mashed dal (if using) and let it cook for 2 more minutes.
    5. Add 1 more cup of water and bring it to a light boil. Do not overboil—just let it froth up.
  • Step 4: Tempering (Tadka)
    1. Heat ghee/oil in a small pan.
    2. Add mustard seeds, let them splutter.
    3. Add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, asafoetida, and curry leaves.
    4. Pour this hot tadka over the rasam and mix well.
  • Step 5: Serve!
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with steamed rice, or drink it as a warm, comforting soup.
